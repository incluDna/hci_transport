# Executive Summary  
Recent studies show mixed results on how AI/bot route suggestions compare with people’s own choices. In tourism contexts, smart routing algorithms (e.g. using social-network or hybrid-trust methods) generally increase travelers’ reported satisfaction with travel time and route quality【76†L60-L68】. For example, one experiment found satisfaction scores rose ~9–10 points (out of 100) after using an AI route recommender【76†L60-L68】. However, user-perceived “overall satisfaction” sometimes favors simpler or familiar routes; in a Kyoto tourist study, a basic (fastest-route) planner gave higher **overall** satisfaction than a sophisticated congestion-aware planner【61†L1059-L1064】. Large language model agents can mimic human choices in simulated congestion games, outperforming traditional models by factors of 2–3【6†L7-L13】, but these are model comparisons rather than human trials. Pedestrian route-choice experiments show additional factors: presenting gradient information shifted choices toward less steep routes【92†L179-L188】, and high “sensation-seeking” users preferred longer easier routes【92†L179-L188】. Key common findings include: *AI-recommended routes often improve objective metrics like travel time or predicted satisfaction【76†L60-L68】【6†L7-L13】, but subjective preference can vary with context. Individual differences (e.g. user preferences, personality) strongly affect route acceptance【92†L179-L188】【85†L169-L177】. Gaps remain in studies directly measuring decision time, trust, or adoption of AI routes. Most experiments focus on satisfaction scores or accuracy, with few on cost or decision latency. Further research should involve diverse modes (e.g. transit, cycling) and measure behavioral trust/adoption alongside satisfaction.

**Common Patterns & Gaps:** Across these studies, AI-route algorithms (whether using social data, multi-agent RL, or multimodal learning) consistently improve objective metrics (accuracy, predicted satisfaction) and user-rated satisfaction in controlled settings【76†L60-L68】【107†L300-L308】. However, actual user *preference* can diverge: travelers sometimes still favor faster or familiar routes, suggesting that AI suggestions must balance efficiency with human biases【61†L1059-L1064】【92†L179-L188】. Personal factors (e.g. familiarity, personality) strongly modulate choices【92†L179-L188】【85†L169-L177】. Notably, **subjective satisfaction and trust** are less studied: one review notes aligning suggestions with user preferences is key to trust【85†L169-L177】, yet few empirical user experiments measure trust or adoption. Similarly, metrics like travel cost or decision time are rarely reported. Overall, the literature shows promise for AI-assisted routing but indicates a need for more user-centric experiments: e.g. A/B trials with AI vs human planners measuring decision time, trust, and long-term adoption.
